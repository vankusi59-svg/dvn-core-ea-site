How to backtest an EA in MetaTrader 5

A disciplined MT5 Strategy Tester workflow covering data range, execution assumptions, costs, result review and the limits of historical tests.

Quy trình Strategy Tester có kỷ luật: dữ liệu, chi phí, điều kiện khớp lệnh, cách đọc kết quả và giới hạn của backtest.

Use this guide to verify process—not to predict performance.Updated 18 July 2026
STEP BY STEP
  1. 01

    Define the question first

    Decide what you are testing: basic operation, parameter sensitivity, broker compatibility or behavior across different market regimes.

  2. 02

    Choose the correct symbol and timeframe

    Match the product specification. A test on the wrong chart configuration does not represent the intended system.

  3. 03

    Use a meaningful date range

    Include varied conditions rather than a short favorable window. Record the exact start and end dates with every result.

  4. 04

    Model realistic trading costs

    Use realistic spread, commission, swap and execution assumptions for the broker environment you expect to use.

  5. 05

    Keep risk settings explicit

    Record the deposit, leverage, lot-sizing mode and maximum exposure. Do not compare tests that use different risk without labeling the difference.

  6. 06

    Review more than net profit

    Inspect drawdown, number of trades, losing sequences, exposure, recovery behavior and whether results depend on a small number of outliers.

  7. 07

    Validate out of sample and on demo

    Treat historical results as research evidence only. Use an unseen period and then a demo forward test before making a deployment decision.

FINAL CHECKLIST

Before you continue.

  • Exact symbol, timeframe and EA version recorded
  • Date range and data quality documented
  • Spread, commission, swap and execution assumptions stated
  • Deposit, leverage and sizing mode disclosed
  • Drawdown and losing sequences reviewed
  • Out-of-sample and demo checks completed

Historical tests and demo operation do not guarantee live results.
