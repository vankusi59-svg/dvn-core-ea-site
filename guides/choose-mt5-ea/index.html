How to choose an MT5 EA by logic and risk

A buyer-focused framework for comparing Expert Advisors by market logic, position model, risk controls, operating conditions and supervision requirements.

Use this guide to verify process—not to predict performance.Updated 18 July 2026
STEP BY STEP
  1. 01

    Name the entry logic

    You should be able to explain whether the EA trades reversals, breakouts, momentum or another defined setup.

  2. 02

    Understand the position model

    Check the maximum number of positions, whether trades are independent and whether any recovery, grid or lot multiplication is possible.

  3. 03

    Inspect the loss architecture

    Confirm how stop loss, lot sizing, daily limits and abnormal-market protections behave before considering the upside.

  4. 04

    Match the market environment

    Verify the intended symbol, timeframe, spread tolerance, VPS expectation and broker execution requirements.

  5. 05

    Assess supervision needs

    Decide how often settings, logs, exposure and product updates must be reviewed. Automation still requires an operator.

  6. 06

    Test before purchasing

    Use the free MQL5 demo in Strategy Tester and reject any system whose behavior or downside you cannot explain.

  7. 07

    Buy through the official listing

    Verify current price, license terms, activations and product updates on the official MQL5 Market page.

FINAL CHECKLIST

Before you continue.

  • Entry logic can be explained in plain language
  • Maximum exposure and position count are known
  • Stop-loss and sizing behavior are understood
  • Broker, symbol and timeframe requirements fit
  • Demo behavior has been reviewed
  • Official MQL5 terms checked before purchase

Historical tests and demo operation do not guarantee live results.
